The book

is very old, very dusty, there are ink smudges, a few circles left from misplaced mugs. Much of the text is hard to read. Opening the cover, there are more or less two sections of decipherable text. Upon closer inspection the first section appears to be a collection of papers not included in binding of the original (perhaps stuffed in separately by the owner?). This section is in the practiced, methodical handwriting (if a bit rushed) of a scribe copying text for sale.. it starts out clean but after the first page, they cling together and it deteriorates rapidly.
Found several decades before the spellweavers... the God's Tears are said to have fueled their glorious ascension. The intent behind their creation is widely debated, ... some romantics may argue they are literally tears of sadness, or perhaps tears of love and happiness, the more sound of mind argue the name is based solely on their bright blue coloration and their occasionally droplet shaped appearance. While neither priests nor bards, nor scholars can agree what inspired the creation of the God's Tears and their gifting to the world. whether benevolent, malicious or some godly event, most of the common folk see the event in a positive light or even as a grand omen; a signal of the righteousness of the Arcane...It cannot be denied, even so far in the past, that their power was immense...
